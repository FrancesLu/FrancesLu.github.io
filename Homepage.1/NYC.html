WELCOME (current) NEST+M LIFE RANDOM NYC PLACES TO VISIT ESSAY

Central Park (59th - 110th Street)

Central Park is one of the largest parks in Manhattan and covers over 840 acres of land!! It's a perfect place to relax or take a nice walk in. From exciting musical performances to zoos to fun family events, Central Park will always have something to keep you company!

Japan Village (Brooklyn)

Japan Village is known for creating an environment that emulates Japanese culture. Located in Brooklyn, you could find instafamous photo places, various restaurants, and a comforting environment. Even though I only went there once, I love the community and cuisines that they offer!

Kinokuniya Bookstore (42nd Street Bryant Park)

This bookstore is notoroious for its wide selection of books ranging from manga to comics to stationaries. Located right by Bryant Park, it is the perfect spot to explore the city while also immersing yourself in different genres of books. Make sure to check it out!

Miniso (Soho)

Miniso is a Japanese inspired retail store that is filled with adorable plushies, cute beauty products, and amazing figurines. It is known for it's $10 and under pricings! If you're anywhere near Soho, you must check this place out, it is perfect for gift giving and filling your heart with joy!

Udon Lab (Koreatown)

Udon Lab, located in Koreatown, is known for its 100% homemade style food. You can find food ranging from edamame to onigiri to bento boxes. Although it can be a bit pricey, you can really taste the authentic flavors that this store provides. If you're ever near this area, you have to check out this amazing restaurant!

Roosevelt Island

Roosevelt Island is located on the F line and is notorious for its spacious and tight-knit community. There is a transportation known as the tramway that connects Roosevelt Island to the Upper East Side of Manhattan. Not only do you get an extraordinary view of the city, but you can explore the endless potential of this small island.

Muji Store (42nd Street)

While there are many Muji stores scattered across the city, I personally love the one located on 42nd Street. It is known for its high-quality and aesthetic stationary items along with its various clothing lines. It's a great place to visit when hanging out with your friends as there is an upper floor and lower floor.
